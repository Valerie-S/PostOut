What is Post Out?

An anonymous open space that enables people with mental illness to share their stories and support each other in an environment without any negative feedback.

Statistics show us...

Youth is the #1 risk population for suicide. The suicide rate of youth in Canada is the 3rd highest among industrialized countries [2]. About 20% of Canadians have developed a mental illness by the age of 25 [1]. Among 15-24-year-olds, suicide causes 24% of all deaths [2]. Youth generally need to wait for at least a year to receive supports and services during a crisis in mental health, and eventually only 1 out of 5 will receive them [2]. Stigma and discrimination further increase the difficulty for youth to receive treatment and community acceptance [2].

PostOut creates a healthy outlet where youth can express their thinking immediately via various media, get support and join scheduled, moderated chat groups. We aim to provide a peer support group to youth without stigma and discrimination. Shared posts are all anonymous. There are no 'like' buttons to stir unnecessary competition and stigma.

The effects of social media online on mental health are mixed. For instance, being involved in online communities reduces the level of stress[4]. However, features such as instant messaging do the opposite[5,6}. PostOut provides a simple platform on which users share and provide supportive feedback without unnecessary features in popular social media. Chat rooms facilitated by experienced moderators are held monthly. We provide an outlet where youth can seek support when professional help is not immediately available.
